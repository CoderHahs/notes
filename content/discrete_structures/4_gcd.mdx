# Lemma

> Let $a,b,q, r \in \Z$ such that $a=b\cdot q + r$  
> Then $\mathrm{gcd}(a,b) = \mathrm{gcd}(b,r)$

# Euclid's Algorithm (a, b)

> $x = a$  
> $y = b$  
> **while** $y \neq 0$  
>  $r = x  \ \mathrm{mod} \ y$  
>  $y = r$  
> **return** $x$

# Bezout's Theorem

> Let $a,b \in \Z^+$.  
> There exists $s,t \in \Z$ such that:  
> $s\cdot a + t\cdot b = \mathrm{gcd}(a,b)$
